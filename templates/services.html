FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and auto repair in general.

For most vehicles, we recommend changing the oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles for conventional oil and every 7,500 to 10,000 miles for synthetic oil. However, this can vary based on your vehicle type, driving conditions, and manufacturer recommendations. Check your owner's manual for specific guidance or consult with our technicians.

If your check engine light comes on, it's important to have your vehicle diagnosed as soon as possible. While it doesn't always indicate a serious problem, ignoring the warning could lead to more extensive damage. Our diagnostic equipment can quickly identify the cause of the check engine light, allowing us to address the issue promptly.

Brake pad replacement intervals vary depending on your driving habits, vehicle type, and the quality of the brake pads. On average, brake pads need to be replaced every 30,000 to 70,000 miles. Warning signs that your brakes need attention include squeaking or grinding noises when braking, a vibrating brake pedal, or the brake warning light appearing on your dashboard.

Tires should be replaced when the tread depth reaches 2/32 of an inch. You can check this using the penny test: insert a penny with Lincoln's head upside down into the tread. If you can see all of Lincoln's head, it's time for new tires. Other signs include uneven wear, bulges or cracks in the sidewall, and excessive vibration while driving. We recommend having your tires inspected regularly during routine maintenance visits.
